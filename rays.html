CatchRays is a brand built around a community of individuals who love the summer, beach, and to be tan.

They wanted to build an iOS exculsive application because they realized there wasn't a solid application that really gave users the upperhand when it came to accurate tracking of UV Index and creating an overall better tanning experience.

Role

Product Designer

Duties

Website Design & Development, UX Research, End-to-End Product Design, Content Management, Front-End Development, Usability Testing, Interaction Design.

View live site

After taking the project, my first task was to create a website for the brands physical products and app download. The brand wanted to draw in people from there overall brand image and corky yet sleek apparel. The plan was simple, direct potential users from social to website for one product, then hook them with an exciting new application that actually solves there issues.

Our wireframes for the site focused on interaction. My biggest challenge was understanding how the elements would engage with users while ensuring the site remained user-friendly and interactive. To tackle this, I employed what I call the 'puzzle method,' designing each section individually and then assembling them to create an optimal user flow. This approach aimed to enhance user engagement, minimize content overload, and deliver the best learning and sales experience.

Learning about our users brought alot of insight and challenges due to the broad range of technology profientecy. Our main mission for our platform had to remain as user friendly as possible while balancing the robust technology our system encompasses.

We initailly set out to design a software that could have a focus on map view utilizing Google Maps. However, we came to realize that our platform could perform best with a focus on showcasing data and having the AI do the heavy lifting with the map functions on the backend. Having the user doing minimal work behind the desk was our finalized goal.

Project Overview:

After completing development, the CEO and I spearheaded outreach to over twelve cities nationwide, successfully securing agreements with five municipalities to implement our finalized product at key intersections. Additionally, we secured federal grants to fund the purchase and installation of new hardware, ensuring an optimal user experience that seamlessly integrated both hardware and software.

Impact:

Within three months, our system demonstrated superior performance over the existing competitor, Synchro Green. We achieved a remarkable 22% increase in traffic efficiency and a 31% improvement in decision-making speed, powered by our AI, Nebula.

My Contribution:

I played a pivotal role in enhancing the system’s efficiency, both internally and externally. Prior to our solution, traffic engineers relied on 10-12 different software tools to complete a single task. I addressed this challenge by developing an all-in-one platform that is intuitive, user-friendly, and accessible to a broad range of users. The most compelling aspect of our design is its scalability and customization. The software can be tailored to meet the unique needs of each city, offering modular components that allow for varying levels of complexity and ease of use, depending on user requirements.
